2017's Best

Big Little Lies
Big Little Lies: Big Little Lies is probably the show that shocked me the most. Initial reviews of the show indicated that it wasn’t going to do well (*cough cough* male critics) but I’m so happy I gave it a shot BECAUSE WOW. Who would’ve thought that a show about moms with five-graders who get involved in a murder would be the most interesting thing to happen on TV in 2017. Directed by Jean-Marc Valleé starring Nicole Kidman, Reese Witherspoon, Shailene Woodley, and Laura Dern, this show tackles domestic abuse, cheating, the aftereffects of rape, whilst showcasing school moms drama is an exhilarating ride on its own.
The Handmaid's Tale
The Handmaid’s Tale: Coming in at a time when the President of one of the world’s superpowers openly insults women, it’s hard to imagine that The Handmaid’s Tale is based on a book released over 3 decades ago. Set in the US after a totalitarian theocratic government takes over, it follows the story of Offred, a woman valued for her ability to still be able to carry a child in her womb, as she is assigned to a family to be the designated bearer of the child of a commander (a concubine in essence) with her primary role is to be fucked in a disturbing ritual including the commander’s wife. Truly horrifying and heavy to watch, The Handmaid’s Tale is a constant reminder of what happens when we let old outdated ideologies to reign in times of tragedy.
Dear White People
Dear White People: A satirical show following a group of black teenagers mainly Sam, the host of a college radio show called Dear White people (she’s dating a white guy!!) is one of the most relevant tv shows of 2017. The first season is written perfectly as it navigates racism in one of the age-old monuments in history: a university. The depiction of the characters in this show is absolutely fantastic: Troy Fairbanks, the son of the dean who one day aspires to be a politician, Lionel a closted-gay black man who just wants to be part of one of the prestigious school papers, Coco, Sam’s former best friend who disagrees with her views and opinions, etc. This show is underrated as hell considering and more people should give it a shot and enjoy and be educated as well.

American Vandal
American Vandal: American Vandal is brilliant. There’s no other way to describe it, this mockumentary gets everything right about what makes a compelling documentary with a seemingly ridiculous premise, who drew the dicks on the 27 faculty cars? It’s funny, it’s gripping, and it even presents you with a thought provoking ending about how if you treat someone like a criminal, they become criminals themselves. Exactly what a true-crime documentary is about. Only it’s about dicks on faculty cars, truly a 2017 gem.
Feud
Feud: Feud is exactly the type of show Peak Ryan Murphy can come up with. Fresh from his acclaimed American Crime Story wins, he comes up with a show detailing the legendary feud of Bette Davis and Joan Crawford. Starring Susan Sarandon and Jessica Lange and showing the incidents from before and during the taping of What Ever Happened to Baby Jane to the events of the 35th Oscars, Feud depicts the mysoginistic horrors of Hollywood and how cruel it was to two aging women. Albeit controversial, Feud was one of the campiest, nuanced, dramatic shows of 2017 and had it not been side by side with the trailblazer that was Big Little Lies, it would’ve surely won all the awards
The Bold Type
The Bold Type: The Bold Type is what Ugly Betty and Sex and The City was to their generations. Executive Producer Joanna Coles, former editor of Cosmopolitan, provides direct input on what might be the most realistic take of the industry at the moment. Following the lives of Jane, Kat, and Sutton, three best friends who work at the fictional Scarlet Magazine as they navigate their careers, struggle with paying the bills, and try to find love. Truly one of the most refreshing shows of 2017.
